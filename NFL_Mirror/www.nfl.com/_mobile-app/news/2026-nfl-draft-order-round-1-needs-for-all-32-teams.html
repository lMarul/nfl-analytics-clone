2026 NFL Draft order: Top 24 picks locked in after Wild Card Weekend

Published: Jan 12, 2026 at 11:17 PM
Dan Parr

Senior Editor, Draft Strategy

This is a look at the first-round order for the 2026 NFL Draft through Wild Card Weekend. The order is determined by record, using strength of schedule as the first tiebreaker (click here to see a full list of the draft-order tiebreakers). Keep in mind -- teams that are still alive in the playoffs are marked as PL (short for playoffs) in the order below. The draft order for playoff teams is determined by the results of postseason play.

2026 NFL Draft order, Picks 1-18

Pick
1
Las Vegas Raiders
3-14

Strength of schedule: .538

Biggest needs: QB, OL, WR, CB, Edge


While there’s a long way to go between now and draft day, it certainly feels like a fait accompli that the Raiders will pick a quarterback in the first round for the first time since 2007.

Pick
2
New York Jets
3-14

Strength of schedule: .552

Biggest needs: QB, OL, CB, WR, DL


The Jets tore things down to the studs at the trade deadline and now have a pile of draft assets to help them reset, including four of the top 50 picks.

Pick
3

3-14

Strength of schedule: .571

Biggest needs: QB, OL, Edge, S, DL


It looks messy in Arizona after the team’s collapse following a 2-0 start to the season. The first priority has to be charting a path forward at the quarterback position, with Kyler Murray’s future murky.

Pick
4
Tennessee Titans
3-14

Strength of schedule: .574

Biggest needs: WR, Edge, CB, OL, S


GM Mike Borgonzi has his work cut out for him. In addition to strengthening Cam Ward’s supporting cast on offense -- a must to set a foundation for the 2025 No. 1 overall pick -- he’ll need to tighten up one of the league’s worst defenses.

Pick
5
New York Giants
4-13

Strength of schedule: .524

Biggest needs: WR, OL, CB, LB, S


Netting the first pick could have led to a franchise-altering windfall for the Giants, but back-to-back wins to close the season dropped them four spots in the order in the last two weeks. The organization can focus on building around a promising young passer in Jaxson Dart.

Pick
6
Cleveland Browns
5-12

Strength of schedule: .486

Biggest needs: QB, OL, WR, CB, LB


With Shedeur Sanders and Dillon Gabriel ranking last and second-to-last in EPA per dropback (min. 200 dropbacks), the Browns’ search for an answer at quarterback continues.

Pick
7
Washington Commanders
5-12

Strength of schedule: .507

Biggest needs: Edge, CB, WR, LB, TE


Things fell apart in an injury-plagued year for Jayden Daniels, and now GM Adam Peters has to pick up the pieces. Giving Daniels more help will be essential, but Dan Quinn will need to fix a defense that hasn’t finished in the top half of the league in scoring on his watch.

Pick
8
New Orleans Saints
6-11

Strength of schedule: .495

Biggest needs: Edge, WR, OL, CB, DL


Whether Tyler Shough is the long-term solution at quarterback remains to be seen, but he did lead the Saints on their longest winning streak since the Drew Brees era. There are plenty of needs on both sides of the ball, from surrounding the QB with more playmakers to adding juice on the defensive line.

Pick

The Chiefs, holding a top-10 pick for the first time since they traded up to draft Patrick Mahomes, have some major question marks to address, starting with Travis Kelce’s future.

Pick

Biggest needs: Edge, OL, CB, DL, S


Cincinnati is holding its highest pick since drafting Ja’Marr Chase fifth overall in 2021. Will the Bengals use it to shore up the Joe Burrow-led offense or fill a hole in a defense that sank in 2025?
